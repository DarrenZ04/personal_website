About Me

I'm an undergraduate at UC Berkeley studying Electrical Engineering & Computer Science with a Data Science minor. I love building end-to-end systems that turn data into decisions—whether that's fantasy basketball draft tools, quantitative trading models, or secure distributed systems.

My interests sit at the intersection of software engineering, machine learning, and analytics. Recently, I've been working on Hooplytics, a fantasy basketball draft optimizer, a quantitative trading pipeline (Quanta), and a custom chess engine. I enjoy tackling hard problems, shipping real products, and learning new technologies along the way.

Hooplytics – Fantasy Basketball Draft Optimizer

Full-stack web application that optimizes fantasy basketball draft strategies using real-time NBA statistics and injury reports. Built a data pipeline to aggregate and standardize player metrics for the 2024–25 season, and implemented algorithms to generate category-based, injury-adjusted player rankings and draft recommendations.

Quanta – Quantitative Trading Model Pipeline

Designed an end-to-end Python pipeline to forecast next-day log-returns across a 40+ ETF sector universe. Integrated OHLCV data from yfinance with macroeconomic series (e.g., VIX, 10-year yield) from FRED and engineered rolling technical indicators, lagged returns, and calendar features. Trained a feed-forward neural network to generate trading signals and evaluate strategy performance.
